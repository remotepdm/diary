Diary of a remote PdM

/ A Piece of Thread

16 Sep 2014

Not a massively productive day yesterday, spent most of it trying to tease out all the ramifications of a particular request, and probably annoying an engineer by not understanding something.

Brought up a few things for me, though. The first is that problems and requests are rarely as simple as they appear at first blush, and it’s my job to ask the right questions to get at a better understanding of an issue’s complexity before I get anywhere near asking an engineer about it. It’s not quite like the 5 whys technique, at least not for me, because you often can’t ask all the necessary questions in a single meeting with someone, you need to go and ruminate on it for a little while, work out what the edge cases might be and what problems they throw up, then go back to that original person.

Second, when working with engineers you really can be speaking a completely different language at times and this can get incredibly frustrating, for both sides. It’s understandable, if you’ve been working with certain systems for a long time it’s not necessarily very easy to put yourself in the position of someone who knows nothing about those systems. I tried the usual things like rephrasing my questions, testing out different (what I believed to be) synonyms to see if they elicited differing responses, but nothing seemed to be working. Part of this was definitely my fault as I persisted in using chat to conduct the conversation when I should have immediately jumped into a video call, especially when I know the engineer in question can come across completely differently in chat and on video.

Partly it’s my ignorance of the technology, though. I’m not the most technical of product managers; I don’t have a CS degree and my speciality lies more in front-end work than the systems architecture that I’m managing, so it will take me a while to get my head around it, but it was definitely a frustrating day.
